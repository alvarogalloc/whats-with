Frequently asked questions

Can I cite the posts?
  1. Start your reference with the last name of the author followed by their initials. If there are multiple authors, separate their names with commas.
  2. List the year of publication in parentheses.
  3. Type the title of the article in sentence case and italicize it.
  4. Add the name of the newspaper or website in title case.
  5. Close with “Available at:” followed by the URL for the article and “Accessed:” followed by the date on which you accessed the website.

For example: Celis Gallo, Santiago. (2023) Article that does not exist. Altamiras Daily. Available at: whats-with.com/dont-try-to-access-this (Accessed: 8 May 2023).

Why do we need to know about the other parts of the world?

As a global newspaper written by people from all over the world, we believe it is important for our readers to know about other parts of the world. By learning about events and issues in different countries and regions, our readers can gain a broader understanding of the world and the diverse perspectives and experiences of people in other places. This knowledge can help to promote empathy and understanding, challenge biases and stereotypes, and foster a more connected global community.


Additionally, many issues and events have global implications and impact, so being informed about what is happening in other parts of the world can help our readers make more informed decisions and engage more effectively as global citizens.

How do you handle plagiarism and fake news?

As a newspaper that accepts news from everyone while still being committed to the integrity of data, we have measures in place to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the information we publish. All submissions are carefully reviewed and fact-checked by our editorial team to verify their accuracy and authenticity. We also have strict guidelines in place to prevent plagiarism and the spread of fake news. By implementing these measures, we strive to provide our readers with trustworthy and reliable information while also giving a platform for diverse voices and perspectives.
